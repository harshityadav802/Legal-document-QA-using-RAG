SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the 1st day of January, 2024, by and between:

ABC Technologies Private Limited, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, having its registered office at 123 Tech Park, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560001, India ("Service Provider");

AND

XYZ Enterprises, a partnership firm registered under the Indian Partnership Act, 1932, having its principal place of business at 456 Commerce Street, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400001, India ("Client").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Service Provider is engaged in the business of providing software development and IT consulting services;

WHEREAS, the Client desires to obtain certain software development services from the Service Provider;

WHEREAS, the Service Provider is willing to provide such services to the Client on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE I
DEFINITIONS

1.1 Definitions. As used in this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

(a) "Confidential Information" means any and all information or data that has or could have commercial value or other utility in the business in which a party is engaged. If Confidential Information is in written form, the disclosing party shall label or stamp the materials with the word "Confidential" or some similar warning.

(b) "Deliverables" means all work product, documents, software, code, reports, analyses, and other materials produced by the Service Provider in connection with the Services.

(c) "Intellectual Property Rights" means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and other intellectual property rights.

(d) "Services" means the software development and IT consulting services described in Schedule A attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference.

(e) "Term" means the period commencing on the Effective Date and ending on December 31, 2024, unless earlier terminated in accordance with Article VII of this Agreement.

1.2 Interpretation. In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires:

(i) references to a "party" or "parties" shall mean a party or the parties to this Agreement;

(ii) headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement;

(iii) words importing the singular include the plural and vice versa.

ARTICLE II
SERVICES

2.1 Scope of Services. The Service Provider shall perform the Services as described in Schedule A. The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards.

2.2 Changes to Services. Any changes to the scope of Services must be agreed upon in writing by both parties. The Service Provider shall not be obligated to perform any services beyond those described in Schedule A without a written change order signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

2.3 Service Standards. The Service Provider shall:

(a) Assign qualified personnel to perform the Services;

(b) Comply with all applicable laws and regulations in performing the Services;

(c) Maintain adequate insurance coverage throughout the Term;

(d) Provide the Client with regular status updates on the progress of the Services.

2.4 Client Cooperation. The Client shall:

(a) Provide the Service Provider with timely access to information, materials, and resources reasonably necessary for the performance of the Services;

(b) Designate a project manager to coordinate with the Service Provider;

(c) Review and approve Deliverables within ten (10) business days of receipt.

ARTICLE III
PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 Fees. In consideration for the Services, the Client shall pay the Service Provider the fees set forth in Schedule B attached hereto ("Fees").

3.2 Payment Schedule. The Client shall pay invoices within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. All payments shall be made in Indian Rupees by bank transfer to the account specified by the Service Provider.

3.3 Late Payment. If the Client fails to pay any amount due within the time specified, the Client shall pay interest on the overdue amount at the rate of 1.5% per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower.

3.4 Taxes. All Fees are exclusive of applicable taxes, including Goods and Services Tax (GST). The Client shall be responsible for all applicable taxes, duties, and charges imposed by government authorities.

3.5 Disputed Invoices. If the Client disputes any invoice in good faith, the Client shall:

(a) Notify the Service Provider in writing within ten (10) days of receipt of the disputed invoice;

(b) Pay any undisputed portion of the invoice by the due date;

(c) Work with the Service Provider in good faith to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days.

ARTICLE IV
INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 Ownership of Deliverables. Subject to full payment of all Fees, the Client shall own all right, title, and interest in and to the Deliverables created specifically for the Client under this Agreement, including all Intellectual Property Rights therein.

4.2 License to Pre-existing Materials. The Service Provider retains ownership of all pre-existing materials, tools, frameworks, and methodologies used in connection with the Services ("Background IP"). The Service Provider hereby grants the Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free license to use the Background IP solely to the extent necessary to use the Deliverables.

4.3 Third-Party Components. The Service Provider shall notify the Client of any third-party software or components incorporated into the Deliverables, including any open-source software, and shall obtain all necessary licenses for the Client's use thereof.

ARTICLE V
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Confidentiality Obligations. Each party agrees to:

(a) Keep the other party's Confidential Information strictly confidential;

(b) Not disclose the other party's Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;

(c) Use the other party's Confidential Information only for the purposes of this Agreement;

(d) Protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.2 Exceptions. The obligations in Section 5.1 shall not apply to information that:

(i) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party;

(ii) was rightfully known to the receiving party before disclosure;

(iii) is independently developed by the receiving party without use of the Confidential Information;

(iv) is required to be disclosed by applicable law or court order, provided the receiving party gives prompt written notice to the disclosing party.

5.3 Duration. The confidentiality obligations under this Article V shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

ARTICLE VI
REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

6.1 Mutual Representations. Each party represents and warrants that:

(a) It has the full power and authority to enter into this Agreement and to perform its obligations hereunder;

(b) This Agreement constitutes a legal, valid, and binding obligation, enforceable against it in accordance with its terms;

(c) The execution and performance of this Agreement does not violate any applicable law or any agreement to which it is a party.

6.2 Service Provider Warranties. The Service Provider represents and warrants that:

(a) The Services shall be performed by qualified personnel with the necessary skills and experience;

(b) The Deliverables shall conform to the specifications set forth in Schedule A;

(c) The Deliverables shall not infringe any third-party Intellectual Property Rights.

6.3 Disclaimer. EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT, THE SERVICE PROVIDER MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING ANY IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

ARTICLE VII
TERMINATION

7.1 Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until December 31, 2024, unless earlier terminated pursuant to this Article VII.

7.2 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other party.

7.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party:

(a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach;

(b) Becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver appointed;

(c) Ceases to carry on its business.

7.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:

(a) The Client shall pay all Fees for Services performed up to the effective date of termination;

(b) Each party shall return or destroy the other party's Confidential Information;

(c) Sections 4, 5, 6.3, 8, and 9 shall survive termination.

ARTICLE VIII
LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

8.1 Indirect Damages. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT.

8.2 Cap on Liability. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID BY THE CLIENT IN THE THREE (3) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO LIABILITY.

8.3 Exceptions. The limitations in this Article VIII shall not apply to:

(a) A party's indemnification obligations under Article IX;

(b) Damages arising from a party's gross negligence or willful misconduct;

(c) Breach of confidentiality obligations under Article V.

ARTICLE IX
INDEMNIFICATION

9.1 Indemnification by Service Provider. The Service Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Client from and against any third-party claims arising from:

(a) The Service Provider's gross negligence or willful misconduct;

(b) Infringement of any third-party Intellectual Property Rights by the Deliverables (excluding any infringement caused by Client-provided materials).

9.2 Indemnification by Client. The Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Service Provider from and against any third-party claims arising from:

(a) The Client's gross negligence or willful misconduct;

(b) The Client's use of the Deliverables in violation of this Agreement.

9.3 Indemnification Procedure. The party seeking indemnification ("Indemnified Party") shall:

(a) Promptly notify the other party ("Indemnifying Party") of any claim;

(b) Cooperate reasonably with the Indemnifying Party in the defense of the claim;

(c) Give the Indemnifying Party sole control of the defense and settlement.

ARTICLE X
GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India, without regard to its conflict of law provisions.

10.2 Dispute Resolution. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be:

(a) First attempted to be resolved by the parties through good faith negotiations for a period of thirty (30) days;

(b) If unresolved, submitted to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 of India;

(c) The seat of arbitration shall be Bengaluru, Karnataka, India;

(d) The arbitration shall be conducted in English.

10.3 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by:

(a) Personal delivery;

(b) Courier service with tracking;

(c) Registered mail; or

(d) Email with confirmation of receipt.

10.4 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, including all Schedules, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions.

10.5 Amendments. No amendment to this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

10.6 Waiver. No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing. No waiver shall constitute a continuing waiver or a waiver of any other provision.

10.7 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

10.8 Assignment. Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, except that either party may assign this Agreement to an affiliate or in connection with a merger or acquisition.

10.9 Force Majeure. Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance due to circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, pandemic, war, or government action, provided the affected party gives prompt written notice.

10.10 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.
